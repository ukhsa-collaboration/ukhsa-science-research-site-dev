Animal Research - Promoting Transparency

The UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) is committed to the responsible use of animals in research and testing, in line with the principles of the 3Rs: Replacement, Reduction and Refinement. We are committed to ensuring that any animal research we undertake is necessary, justified and conducted to the highest ethical standards.

Explore UKHSA research

Why do we use animals in our research?

Alongside epidemiological and cell studies, animal research plays an essential role in understanding how infectious, radiological, chemical and other environmental agents impact human health (Figure 1). The biological similarities between humans and other species means that they can, on some occasions, be the only effective model for research where responses to agents is too complex to be modelled in any other way. Such animal models provide key scientific evidence that informs interventions, policies and responses to local, national and international health security incidents, protecting the population from existing and emerging health threats.
